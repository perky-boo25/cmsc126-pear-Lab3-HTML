A playlist that feels like me on a regular day. A little chaotic, a little depressed, a little dramatic (you know they’re not “little” at all), but I’m still functioning. erine's jar
At a jazz cafe where Olivia Dean’s songs were playing

Recently, Olivia Dean has become one of my favorite artists, and it honestly feels a little scary how much I relate to her songs as if she wrote those songs dedicated to me. What really sticks with me is her album “The Art of Loving” — it explores love in ways that it is not just a perfect fairytale, but a deep ocean of confusion, mess, and vulnerability. There’s something about the way she frames love as something soft and human but also dramatic or chaotic. Listening to it feels like being reminded that loving, in all its forms, is an art we’re constantly learning, and that there’s something beautiful about simply feeling that deeply.
adrienne's jar
AURORA HAIK Concert, Full Performance

AURORA has been my top artist for years. One thing I admire about her is how she somehow always outperforms her studio recordings in her live performances. This concert is one of the best examples of that. It features one of the most powerful renditions of “The Seed” — a song that is both politically relevant and deeply moving — performed alongside a remarkably talented band and choir. I highly recommend everyone give it a listen. It’s genuinely a piece of art; sometimes it’s hard to believe it’s live. With the voice and heart of an angel, I am never ashamed to support her. What an ethereal person to love.

One of my favorite setlists that I can listen to regardless of what I’m doing. I chose this playlist not just because I enjoy the music, but because I’m passionate about DJing. I like studying how tracks flow together, how energy builds, and how transitions are structured.
